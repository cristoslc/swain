# Locke and Latham's Goal-Setting Theory

## Core Finding

- Specific, difficult goals consistently enhance motivation and performance compared to vague or easy goals
- Effect sizes range from .42 to .80 in meta-analyses
- Performance is up to 250% higher with difficult goals versus easy goals
- Supported by nearly 400 lab and field studies, across 88 tasks and 40,000+ participants

## Goal-Performance Relationship

- Linear relationship between goal difficulty and performance — up to the point where ability limits are reached
- When goals exceed ability limits, performance drops — the sweet spot is challenging but attainable
- Goals serve a directive function: they direct attention toward goal-relevant activities and away from irrelevant ones

## Five Principles

- **Clarity** — goals must be specific and unambiguous to guide behavior effectively
- **Challenge** — goals must be sufficiently difficult to engage effort and attention
- **Commitment** — individuals must accept and internalize the goal for it to drive performance
- **Feedback** — goals without feedback provide no mechanism for course correction; feedback is essential
- **Task Complexity** — for complex tasks, goals should include strategies, not just outcomes

## Implications

- Goal-setting is one of the most empirically validated theories in organizational psychology
- The directive function means that well-set goals naturally reduce decision fatigue by constraining the relevant option space
- Feedback loops are non-negotiable — without them, goal pursuit becomes unmoored
