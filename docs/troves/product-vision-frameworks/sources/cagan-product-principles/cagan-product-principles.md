# Product Principles (Marty Cagan / INSPIRED)

## Definition

Product principles are foundational beliefs and values that guide product development. They "complement the product vision and strategy by outlining the core beliefs and values guiding the development of a product or product line."

They speak to the **nature** of the products you want to create — not features, not releases, not tied to any timeframe.

## Relationship to Vision and Strategy

Three elements work together:
- **Product Vision**: Describes the aspirational future state
- **Product Strategy**: Outlines the sequence of releases to achieve the vision
- **Product Principles**: Define core beliefs shaping HOW products are developed

Principles are "aligned with the product vision for an entire product line, providing a long-term foundation for development decisions."

Cagan notes principles are "often lumped together and just referred to as 'the product vision'" — meaning they're typically created alongside the vision and treated as part of the same exercise.

## Key Characteristics

- Inspired by company and team core values
- May inspire specific product features but are NOT a list of features
- Enduring — not tied to timeframes or releases
- Serve as decision-making guides for teams
- A good set of principles should help teams make hard tradeoffs

## The eBay Example

eBay's marketplace generated most revenue from sellers, creating a strong incentive to please sellers. But the company realized the real reason sellers loved eBay was because it provided them with buyers.

This led to a critical product principle: **"In cases where the needs of the buyers and the sellers conflict, we will prioritize the needs of the buyer, because that's actually the most important thing we can do for sellers."**

This principle:
- Resolved a recurring tension (whose needs win?)
- Was counterintuitive (prioritize the non-revenue side)
- Guided specific decisions without prescribing features
- Remained durable across many product iterations
