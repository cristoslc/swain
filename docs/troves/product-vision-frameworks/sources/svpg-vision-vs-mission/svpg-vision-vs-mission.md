# Product Vision vs. Mission (Marty Cagan / SVPG)

## Mission Statement
A pithy slogan describing a company's purpose. Example: "organize the world's information." Concise, memorable, answers "what is this company about?"

## Product Vision
A persuasive, inspiring tool that guides product teams toward a specific future state. Fundamentally different from a mission.

### What a Good Vision Accomplishes
- Keeps focus on the customer rather than internal processes
- Provides a North Star offering common understanding across all teams
- Inspires ordinary people to create extraordinary products
- Demonstrates meaningful impact on users' lives (vs. mere feature lists)
- Leverages industry trends and technologies to solve problems in newly possible ways

### Indirect Benefits
- Enables engineering to plan architecture for future needs
- Drives team topology decisions
- Combined with annual objectives, shapes product strategy
- Powerful recruitment tool
- Evangelism tool for securing organizational support

## Common Mistakes
1. **Confusing vision with mission** — a purpose slogan is not a product vision
2. **Multiple team visions** — when each team creates its own vision, they lose the unifying North Star. "Everyone picking out their own star from the sky."

## Characteristics of Effective Visions
- Compelling and inspiring, not literal specifications
- Persuasive in nature ("a bit of an art form")
- Not overly detailed or prescriptive
- Emotionally resonant — often dramatized through videos or "visiontypes"
