# Product Vision FAQ (Marty Cagan / SVPG)

## Timeframe
- Software: 2-5 years
- Device companies: 5-10 years
- Common mistake: 1-year horizons (that's a roadmap, not a vision)

## Scope
Broader than a single team, narrower than the entire enterprise. A marketplace needs one unified vision, not separate buyer/seller visions.

## Ownership
Head of product crafts it. CEO should feel genuine ownership and evangelize it.

## Right Level of Detail
Must move beyond generic statements. Should give audiences "a real sense of how you will make their lives better" while acknowledging uncertainty in execution.

## Development Process
1-2 day facilitated sessions with 5-15 key leaders. Essential inputs:
- Business objectives and constraints
- Customer problems and critical insights
- Enabling technologies
- Industry trends and competitive landscape
- Go-to-market considerations
- Organizational capabilities

## Maintenance
- Strategy changes yearly/quarterly; visions remain stable for several years
- Rare pivots when discovery uncovers substantially larger opportunities
- More common: premature abandonment (giving up after 6-12 months)
- Failure: after sustained effort (1+ year), realize customers don't need the problem solved

## 10 Key Principles
1. Start with Why
2. Fall in love with the problem, not the solution
3. Skate to where the puck is heading
4. Be stubborn on the vision, flexible on the details (Bezos)
5. Should be emotional and inspiring
6. Challenge should be worthy of time and effort
7. Customer-centric — focus on improving customers' lives
8. Leverage enabling technologies
9. Single unifying vision across teams
10. Persuasive presentation (visiontypes, videos)
