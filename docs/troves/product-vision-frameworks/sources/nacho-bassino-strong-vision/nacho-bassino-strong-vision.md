# How to Craft a Strong Product Vision (Nacho Bassino)

## Definition
"A concrete view of how we plan to deliver on the company's mission through the product" (citing Cagan).

## Four Essential Attributes
1. **Customer-centric** — focused on future value for customer needs, not internal capabilities
2. **Inspirational** — ambitious and bold, connects teams to purpose, attracts missionaries over mercenaries
3. **Differentiated** — articulates why YOUR organization can uniquely pursue this
4. **Stretch but attainable** — 3-5 year horizon, ambitious without being abstractly unrealistic

## Formats
- **Storyboards** — graphical narratives with user as protagonist, from painful present to successful future
- **Visiontypes** — static visual prototypes of an inspiring future state
- **Vision videos** — comprehensive multimedia versions

## Critical Questions
1. Who is the target user?
2. What needs or use cases matter most?
3. Where do these needs emerge and get resolved?
4. What is the medium?
5. How does your solution differentiate?

## Common Pitfall
Avoid template-based approaches that force equal emphasis on irrelevant topics. Vision relevance varies by product type.
