# Tenets: The Writing Super-Decider (Doc Bar Raiser)

## Definition

"Tenets are a set of principles or beliefs that are there to guide decision making."

Key distinction: a charter describes WHAT a team does; tenets describe HOW they accomplish it.

## Role in Decision-Making

Tenets serve as organizational anchors preventing teams from rehashing the same discussions. They resolve ambiguity by establishing clear priorities and trade-offs.

Example: when a team couldn't decide whether to customize content for multiple roles, introducing a tenet stating the program's primary focus ended the recurring debate and enabled decisive action.

## Characteristics

- Emphasize priorities — can be written as value trade-offs (e.g., "speed over accuracy")
- Evolve over time — "unless you know better ones"
- Come in types: foundational (why something exists) or aspirational (future operations)
- Drive alignment across organization

## Two Types

**Foundational** — explaining why something exists
**Aspirational** — describing how the team intends to operate in the future

## Example Tenets for a Writing Team

1. Prioritize timely "good enough" documents over perfect late ones
2. Documents must stand alone without supplementary materials
3. Documents initiate decisions, not end projects
4. If meetings lack clear decision drivers, write framing documents instead
5. Welcome feedback as a tool for continuous improvement
