# Vision vs. Strategy (Marty Cagan / SVPG)

## The Hierarchy
1. **Product Vision** — where we ultimately want to go (2-5 years)
2. **Product Strategy** — what problems to solve to get there
3. **Product Discovery** — tactics to solve those problems
4. **Product Delivery** — building the solutions

## Vision
- Describes the desired future state
- Primary purpose: inspire and set direction
- "Stubborn on the vision, flexible on the details" (Bezos)
- Forms: storyboard, narrative (white paper), or visiontype (prototype)
- Validation: some demand validation possible, but fundamentally requires conviction, not certainty

## Strategy
- How to make the vision reality while meeting organizational needs
- Four components: tough choices, insights/leverage, actionable initiatives, active management
- Many companies have goals and roadmaps but no strategy in between

## Key Distinction
"Leadership inspires and sets the direction, and management helps us get there." Vision = leadership. Strategy = management.

## Why Both Matter
- Vision without strategy = inspiration without execution
- Strategy without vision = activity without direction
- "Trying to please everybody will almost certainly please nobody" — strategy prevents delivering the entire vision at once
