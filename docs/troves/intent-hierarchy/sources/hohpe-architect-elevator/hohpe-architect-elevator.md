# The Architect Elevator -- Visiting the Upper Floors

**By Gregor Hohpe | Published 24 May 2017**

## Overview

This article presents the "architect elevator" metaphor -- a framework for understanding how architects must bridge the gap between an organization's executive leadership and technical teams. Hohpe argues that modern architects should move fluidly between organizational levels rather than making isolated design decisions.

## Key Arguments

### Traditional Architecture Roles Are Outdated

Hohpe agrees with Martin Fowler and Erik Doernenburg that "most of what architects traditionally did should be done by developers, by tools, or not at all." The shift requires architects to engage at organizational levels beyond just technical design.

### The Core Metaphor

Large organizations suffer from separation between strategy (penthouse) and execution (engine room), creating communication breakdowns. Architects must ride the elevator between floors to align business strategy with technical reality.

### Six Main Responsibilities

**1. Runtime Architecture Mastery**
Modern distributed, cloud-based applications demand expertise in scalability, monitoring, and deployment -- not just design patterns.

**2. Automate Software Manufacturing**
The real industrialization opportunity lies in automating delivery, not design. This directly impacts business value realization.

**3. Defer Irreversible Decisions**
Minimizing up-front commitments when uncertainty is highest reduces risk. Horizontal scaling and modularity enable later, better-informed decisions.

**4. Frame Architecture as Options**
Presenting architecture through financial option concepts helps executives understand its value -- "you invest in architecture so you can change your mind later."

**5. Context Determines Fitness**
Architecture is "fit or unfit for purpose," not universally good or bad. Context requires visiting multiple organizational levels.

**6. Validate Through Feedback**
Architects must experience consequences of their decisions to improve them. The "ivory tower" anti-pattern lacks crucial feedback mechanisms.

### Organizational Evolution Matters

Technology innovations (DevOps, cloud, automation) only succeed when organizational structures evolve alongside them. Architects must address "people stuff" and systemic bottlenecks like synchronization points and excessive layering.

### ArchOps Teams

Vertical architecture teams spanning multiple organizational levels ensure feedback loops and execution capability. Shared on-call duty with project teams grounds architects in reality.

## Conclusion

Effective architects balance technical depth with organizational influence. Success requires regularly riding the elevator -- maintaining both penthouse connections and engine room expertise -- rather than becoming detached from either realm.
