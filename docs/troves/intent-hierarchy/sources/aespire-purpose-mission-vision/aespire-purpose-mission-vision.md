# The Difference Between Purpose, Mission, and Vision

**Author:** Brian Sooy, Aespire
**Originally published:** November 22, 2013

## The Substitution Test

Substitute the words Why, How, and What for Purpose, Mission, and Vision:

- **Why = Purpose** (The reason we exist): the deeply-held values and beliefs that inspire an organization to make a difference
- **What = Vision** (The goal): the destination at a point in the near or distant future; what you'll experience when you arrive
- **How = Mission** (The path): the unique way you do what you do, the decisions you make to get to your destination

## Purpose

Purpose is the reason your organization has begun a journey, guided by the deeply-held values and beliefs that inspire it to make a difference. Your organization's purpose is the reason it exists beyond making money.

Gary Burnison, CEO of Korn Ferry: "Purpose precedes the first step. The more people embrace the purpose, the more likely they will follow — not only at the beginning when adrenaline is pumping, but later on when the going gets tough."

Purpose focuses on four elements of impact:
1. Why do you believe you can make a difference? — Purpose needs a reason.
2. Who do you want to create impact for? — Purpose needs people.
3. How do you achieve the impact? — Purpose needs a plan.
4. What will impact look like when you achieve it? — Purpose needs vision.

## Vision

Vision is your destination at a point in the near or distant future. Vision defines your goal and sets the expectation of what you'll experience when you arrive. Vision aligns leaders and inspires followers. Vision keeps you on course, to fulfill your purpose.

## Mission

Your mission is the path: the unique way you do what you do, the decisions you make to get to your destination. Mission is doing what matters and eliminating the distractions; it activates the strategy that delivers results and impact.

Will you follow a focused and direct path; or one that wanders? A mission-driven path will be direct, accomplishing the things that matter that align your company goals with your purpose.

## The Relationship

Purpose keeps you focused on why you exist, vision aligns you with your goal, and mission empowers how you will accomplish it.

A mission statement and vision statement are best suited for internal organizational guidance. Purpose is the foundation of mission and vision, but impact is what matters.

## Key Distinction

Great brands don't start with purpose. They begin with focus and alignment: Who you are, what you do, and why it matters. Without a vision, you'll never stay focused. Without purpose, your brand will not stay on mission.

Your company's values and purpose are best shared through a compelling narrative and stories, not your mission statement.
