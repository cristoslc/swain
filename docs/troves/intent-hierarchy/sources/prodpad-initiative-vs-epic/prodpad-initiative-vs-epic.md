# Initiative vs Epic — What's the Difference?

**Author:** Janna Bastow, ProdPad
**Published:** March 7, 2024

## Key Caveat

The terminology you use isn't as important as having a shared understanding of what each term means, how they fit together, and how you go about using them.

## Definitions

### What's an Objective?

Objectives represent your higher-level goals, sitting above your initiatives. They're there to guide the Product team, to help them decide what they should focus on over a particular period. Your objectives will have various initiatives that you take on to achieve them.

### What's an Initiative?

An initiative is generally a larger-scale effort that aligns with your strategic goals for your product or your business. Each initiative is most commonly based on a problem area to solve, an opportunity to tackle, or an area to go after.

People sometimes call them "themes", but this term is somewhat outdated. Themes have evolved into the concept of initiatives.

### What's an Epic?

Epics are one level down from initiatives. If the objectives are like saying "Why should we do this initiative?", and an initiative is asking "What's the problem we're solving?", then your epics are where you ask **"How might we solve this problem?"**

Epics can still be considered large bodies of work, but they're related to a particular feature, a particular solution, or a particular way of tackling things.

### Epics Should Be Experiments

By thinking of your epics as experiments, you're not constantly driven towards just having a bunch of features on your roadmap. Rather, you've got the flexibility to find new, innovative ways to solve your customers' problems.

An experiment could be building a feature, but the best way to solve a problem could also be to take away a feature or change a feature.

## The Decision Stack

Hat tip to Martin Eriksson's work on the product decision stack. The hierarchy:

```
Vision
  Strategy
    Objectives / Goals
      Initiatives        ← "What problem are we solving?"
        Epics            ← "How might we solve it?"
          User Stories   ← "What specifically do we build?"
```

Go down the ladder by asking "How?" — go up the ladder by asking "Why?"

Example:
- Objective: Get more revenue
- How? Build a profitable community → **Objective**
- How? Start by building this part of it → **Initiative**
- How? Let's try this experiment → **Epic**

Climbing back up: Why are we doing this login field? Because we're building the front-end of our community... and so on.

## Initiative Scope

While an initiative could be big enough to span various time horizons, it often makes more sense to break it into smaller pieces (V1, V2, V3). "Build a profitable community" is too big for a single initiative — that's actually an objective that feeds multiple initiatives.

## Epics and Initiatives

Epics normally fall under one initiative. You might have an epic related to multiple initiatives for various reasons, but generally you have initiatives covering wider time spans with epics underneath.

## Alignment

Everything should be driven by business outcomes, objectives, and desired outcomes. You should be **outcome-driven** — not "customer-driven" or "data-driven" but *informed* by data and customers.

## Themes Are Out, Initiatives Are In

The term "initiative" in its current context is newer, solidifying around what people previously called themes. "Themes" is popular in the Agile world but easily misconstrued.
