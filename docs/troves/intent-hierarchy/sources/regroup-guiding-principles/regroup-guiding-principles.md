# Guiding Principles: The "How" Behind Your Strategy

**Author:** Trista Taylor, Regroup
**Published:** November 14, 2024

## Core Definition

While an organization's purpose defines its *why* — the impact an organization strives to make in the world — strategy defines the *how*. It's about making deliberate choices: deciding how to pursue your purpose and tackle challenges by prioritizing one approach over another.

At its core, effective strategy has two essential elements:
1. **Guiding principles** — which provide a framework for decision-making and action
2. **Strategic big bets** — bold, ambitious initiatives that align with those principles to drive meaningful progress

A guiding principle serves as the fundamental approach for how an organization tackles its strategic challenges. More than just aspirational words, it forms the backbone of every major decision, providing a clear and consistent direction.

## Principles vs. Values vs. Goals

Guiding principles are distinct from core values or operational goals — they define the non-negotiable approach that sets a company apart in pursuing its mission.

## Real-World Examples

- **"Everyday Low Prices"** — Walmart. Not merely a slogan; it's a guiding approach that shapes every decision across the organization, from sourcing to supply chain to customer experience.
- **"Cause No Unnecessary Harm"** — Patagonia prioritizes sustainability and environmental responsibility.
- **"Community Focused Growth"** — Airbnb builds trust between hosts, guests, and communities.
- **"Cultivate Connection"** — Starbucks creates inclusive, welcoming spaces.
- **"Affordable Vision for All"** — Warby Parker delivers high-quality, stylish eyewear at accessible prices.

## Why Guiding Principles Matter

Guiding principles are more than a mission statement; they are a playbook for action. When properly defined, they become particularly valuable during times of uncertainty, offering teams a reliable compass for strategic decisions.

Case study: A technology company facing a classic strategic dilemma (aggressive growth vs. product quality) developed "Accelerate and Focus Innovation" as their guiding principle. This balanced both imperatives: it emphasized speed while establishing clear quality parameters. Impact:
- Teams gained clarity on when to push features to market quickly
- Innovation efforts became more focused on high-value initiatives
- Leaders could confidently say "no" to unnecessary quality improvements that would delay delivery

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Too broad or too specific:** The more traits you try to include ("quality," "innovation," "customer focus"), the less actionable the principles become.
2. **Confusing principles with aspirations:** "Be the most innovative in the industry" is destination-focused and self-centered. True guiding principles illuminate the path forward — they articulate specific approaches for navigating challenges and creating value. They serve as a practical compass for daily decision-making, not just an expression of future ambitions.

## Principles and Strategy

When done well, a guiding principle becomes a reliable lens for strategic decision-making. The question: "How will this principle shape your response to a business challenge?" — encourages leaders to think about guiding principles as tools, not just ideals.

Principles aren't set in stone. They need to be revisited periodically as the landscape changes. Revisit at least annually, ideally as part of the strategic planning process.
