After failing to get details about a user who said “TSA sucks and we all know it,” via a traditional court order, DHS is now dragging Reddit in front of a [grand jury](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/04/trump-admin-hounds-reddit-to-reveal-identity-of-user-who-criticized-ice/). The government has grown [increasingly aggressive](https://www.theverge.com/policy/879300/the-dhs-is-reportedly-pressing-social-media-platforms-for-info-about-ice-critics) in its attempts to deal with online critics. Reddit has not said whether it plans to fight the subpoena, but according to * The Intercept*:

“Privacy is central to how Reddit operates, and we take our commitment to protecting that seriously,” the company said in a statement to The Intercept. “We do not voluntarily share information with any government, especially not on users exercising their rights to criticize the government or plan a protest.”
